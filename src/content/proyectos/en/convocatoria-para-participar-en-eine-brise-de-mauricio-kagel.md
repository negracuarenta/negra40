## Open call: 111 cyclists

To take part in *Eine Brise* (1996), by Mauricio Kagel.

*Eine Brise* (A Breeze) is a fleeting action for 111 bicycles. The piece calls for 111 cyclists to fit their bikes with bells or horns, so that together we conjure a sonic mass on two wheels.

The action will take place on Saturday, December 17, 2016, at 7:30 pm, in the vicinity of the MAR Museum of Contemporary Art, Mar del Plata, as part of the AMPLIACIÓN program of activities for the Joseph Beuys Conference Days.

Participants in *Brise* should have:

- a bicycle (fitted with a bell or a horn).
- a kickstand on the bicycle (or some way to keep it upright when not in motion).
- the ability to ride a bicycle.
- full commitment and willingness to take part.
- Note: the general rehearsal will be held that same Saturday the 17th at 6:00 pm, and attending it will be essential to performing in *Brise*.
- No musical knowledge is required to take part in *Brise*.

Direction: **Martín Virgili**
Assistant directors: **Mariano Losi and Jorge Ihlenfeld**

## About the piece

The composition is based on L. V. Beethoven's Piano Sonata No. 32 in C minor, Op. 111, linking the opus number (111) precisely to the number of cyclists. The coincidence holds up — or rather, amuses us. Mar del Plata is a city built for two wheels: the edges of its streets invite you to move through it in balance.

But this noble way of getting around still finds no counterpart among motor vehicles. *Brise* is a call to attention, an encounter of positions, a way of raising one's voice against the dangers of the street.

Performed in more than 50 of the world's major cities, Kagel's composition invites us to be part of a music that is a gesture, and that is also something like the wind. What do you say? Who's in?
