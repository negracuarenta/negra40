## Note

On Saturday, November 28, writer and performer Guillermo Lema will visit Mar del Plata with a program that lets us appreciate both facets of his art.

The concert is also a landmark in terms of its repertoire and authors. A case in point is the premiere of **Verso**, a composition by Argentine graphic artist and designer America Sanchez, written for voices, choir, and string instruments between 1968 and 1982.

The same program will feature the **Teorema Fundamental** (1966), by the La Plata-based artist Edgardo-Antonio Vigo (1928–1997). Vigo's imagery cannot be moored to the dock of any single poetics. His mathematical poems move freely between the visual, the textual, and the musical, which is precisely why they call for new strategies of staging. Under these conditions, it becomes possible to reposition his **Teorema Fundamental** as a poetic-sound action. Along the same lines are the works of another poet and experimental artist, this time from Barcelona: Joan Brossa (1919–1998). Brossa was one of the most prominent avant-garde artists of his time, championing, like Vigo, a multi-focal body of work that eludes any ordering taxonomy. The contemporaneity and the parallels between the two artists regain force in light of the social and artistic dynamics of a good decade and a half into the 21st century. We see their work falling from the sky of texts with an inescapable symbolic force — whether because its political content is regaining relevance, or because its aesthetics speaks to the unspecific movement of the artworks of today.
