**La caída del Marcelina de Ciriza / The Fall of the Marcelina de Ciriza**
A staged concert for clarinet and zurupanga ensemble

**Project:** Sebastián Flores, Martín Virgili

Playa Constitución / Museo MAR, Mar del Plata — 2021

Selected by the Fondo Nacional de las Artes.

[Download the premiere program (PDF)](https://drive.google.com/file/d/1xAPxzKl-SnhnxEtX_zRBqypBvXzzPmWP/view?usp=sharing)

## Presentation

In 1991, the *Marcelina de Ciriza* sailed 15 kilometers from the port of Mar del Plata to the beaches of Constitución with no crew aboard. Local legend holds that only a dog and ghosts were on board. Thirty years after the tragedy — and the harrowing storm that seems to have set the ship's dark departure in motion — the story is still alive in the imagination of Mar del Plata, carrying a sonic and narrative presence that gives rise to this staged concert for clarinet and zurupanga ensemble.

At a macro-formal level, the concert unfolds across two areas: one inside the hall, one outside. The first takes place on the beach framing the Museo MAR, the critical point where the Marcelina de Ciriza lies: from there, the soloist and the composer will paddle out on a surfboard, retracing the ship's final position. The second, inside the hall with an ensemble formed specially for the premiere, rebuilds — sonically and musically — the epic of that sinking.

## Musical movements

The rising of the wind · Theme of the Marcelina · The perfect storm · Sailing among shadows · The secret report · Homage to Rachmaninoff · Running aground · The waves · Dialogue between a dog and a whale · The swallowed cathedral.
