## Notes

Magnetism (for artists)
by **Rodolfo Borzi**

PhD in physics. CONICET researcher.
Magnetism commonly evokes the almost magical possibility of acting on something without actually touching it. I will use this idea as a starting point and a pretext to discuss the physicist's view of action at a distance, the origin of magnetic phenomena, the link between magnetism and light, and physics and mathematics. We will also talk about scientific research in the field of magnetism.

**Duo Stepančić-Gidron**

The duo, based in the Netherlands/New York, presents a program of works that highlight the acoustic properties of the piano — its clear resonance and decay of sound — complemented or reinforced by sine waves, forming an in-situ way in which both sonorities feed and reinforce one another.

Teodora Stepančić – piano
Assaf Gidron – sine waves and sound engineering.

Teodora Stepančić (Serbia) teodora.stepancic.com
She is a pianist and composer. As a pianist she specialized in contemporary music, and has premiered works by various emerging composers. She is a member of Ensemble Modelo62 (Netherlands) and Ensemble Studio6 (Serbia). She forms part of the duo Stepančić-Gidron and frequently collaborates with different contemporary-music ensembles in Europe. As a composer, she has explored several paths incorporating theatricality and visual and performative elements.

Assaf Gidron (Israel) assafgidron.com
He is a composer, performer, and sound designer for film. Born in Tel Aviv, where he was part of the experimental electronic music scene, he studied composition in London and The Hague before settling in Brooklyn in 2014. His recent works focus on the design of economical (simple) setups that elevate lyrical gestures.
