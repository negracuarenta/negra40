We have long walked to awaken the earth and to awaken ourselves. We walk as a practice of resistance, forging an anti-economy of actions and acts. We walk to silence the automatic purring of the mind, and to listen to the vital pulse of everything that surrounds and calls to us. We walk to find ourselves in an integral silence, within a landscape that we look at and that looks back at us.

To listen and to walk.
