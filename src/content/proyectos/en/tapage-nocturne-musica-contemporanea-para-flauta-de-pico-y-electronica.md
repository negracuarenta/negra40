**Joan Izquierdo** was born in Barcelona in 1966. He studied recorder at the Conservatori de Barcelona and later at the Sweelinck Conservatorium in Amsterdam. He currently teaches recorder at the Conservatori Municipal de Barcelona. His commitment to teaching and to spreading the instrument has led him to write articles and textbooks for teaching recorder in schools, alongside an intense schedule of master classes and lectures. As a performer, his work is broad and specialized; he is part of various chamber ensembles addressing styles and interests ranging from 14th- and 15th-century music (**trío Subtilior, Virdung**), to 16th-century polyphony (**Sforzinda** – a recorder quintet), to the performance of Baroque music (**Othello Ensemble**). But perhaps his most influential activity has been in contemporary music, where his persistence has relaunched the instrument in the eyes of composers, having premiered more than fifty newly created works for recorder. In this field he is part of the "**quartet de bec Frullato**," a duo with percussionist **Pilar Subirà**, and maintains a tireless solo career, frequently accompanied by electronics.

**Saratoga trio (members)**

**Nubia Bado**. She completed her musical studies at the Conservatorio Superior de Música de la Ciudad de Buenos Aires "Ástor Piazzolla," where she studied her instrument with maestros Andrés Gerszenzon and Gabriel Pérsico, earning her advanced music teaching degree in 2009. During 2010–2011 she studied at the Conservatorio Superior de Música "Manuel Castillo" (Seville, Spain), studying with maestro Vicente Parrilla. In 2013 she earned a degree in musical arts from the Universidad Nacional de las Artes (UNA). She has taken numerous courses and seminars in recorder and early music in Argentina, Spain, and Brazil, some taught by maestros Pierre Hamon, Joan Izquierdo, Ernesto Schmied, Rodrigo Calveira, and Fernando Paz, among others. As a performer she has appeared in major auditoriums and theaters (Museo Fernández Blanco, Museo Histórico Nacional, Museo Sarmiento, Salón Dorado de Legislatura, Teatro Avenida, Teatro Empire, etc.) and has also given educational concerts in various institutions. She currently teaches at conservatories and music schools and performs with different early-music ensembles and projects. She is also a permanent member of groups such as Saratoga trío (contemporary music), Capilla Profana (Renaissance), and La Manfredina (medieval).

**Graciela Flores**. She completed her musical studies at the Universidad Nacional de las Artes (UNA), pursuing a recorder degree with Prof. Gabriel Pérsico, and took master classes in recorder in Argentina with Joan Izquierdo (Barcelona), Ricardo Kanji (Brazil), and Amadeo Lechner (France), as well as abroad with professors Christoph Ehrsam (Curitiba Early Music Festival, Brazil), David Antich (Morella Medieval and Renaissance Music Festival, Spain), Miako Klein (master class, Berlin), and classes in tin whistle and traditional Irish music at the Willie Clancy Festival (Ireland). She is part of various chamber ensembles that have performed in major music series and venues around the country: the Fundación OSDE Contemporary Music Cycle, Auditorio de Belgrano, Teatro Niní Marshall, Teatro Auditorium (Mar del Plata), the Shakespeare Festival (2012–2013–2014 editions), the "Ciclo Bares Notables" (2014), and educational concerts for cooperatives and university extension projects. She currently teaches music and recorder at educational institutions.

**Marcos Meroni**. He completed his musical studies at the Conservatorio Superior de Música de la Ciudad de Buenos Aires "Astor Piazzolla" (advanced teaching degree) with Mtro. Luis Cosla, and at the Universidad Nacional de las Artes (UNA, degree in musical arts) with Andrés Gerszenzon as thesis advisor. He took courses and seminars in Rome, in Galicia, and in Buenos Aires with maestros Joan Izquierdo, Pierre Hamon, Ernesto Schmied, Carlos Nuñez, Daniel Bellón, among others. He has taught various courses in the city of Buenos Aires and in Santa Fe. He has performed with a variety of classical and popular music ensembles in cities such as Temuco, Valparaíso (Chile), La Plata, Bahía Blanca, Entre Ríos, Mar del Plata, Mar de Ajó, and Buenos Aires. He has appeared in museums and theaters including the Buenos Aires City Legislature, Teatro Astral, ND Ateneo, Avenida, Margarita Xirgu, The Roxy Live, the Municipal Theater of Bahía Blanca, Premiere, Argentino de La Plata, Fundación de Cultura Osde, etc. He is also devoted to the study of the bagpipe and Galician culture. He was part of the group *Xeito Novo* (*Celtic folk*), and currently plays with *Herbadaboa* (traditional Galician music) and *Saratoga Trío*. He teaches and has given educational concerts at various institutions.
